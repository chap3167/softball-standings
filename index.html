Recap Sat 4/18 · 6U Alo · Division Showdown

14-13 Isn't a Coincidence Anymore.

Aggies 14 – 13 Comets SWMS Field 4

Nobody came to Field 4 this morning expecting a coronation. The Aggies walked in 7-0, the Comets walked in 6-1 — one mile between their elementary schools and every advanced metric pointing at the Comets as the team Alo should fear most. Bunker Hill had the record; Frostwood had the .784 Pythagorean and a +31 run differential. Only one of those numbers actually decides a softball game.

The game itself was the loudest the Aggies have played. The Comets led through the middle innings. Bunker Hill kept answering — and kept finding one more run. Final 14-13, the closest margin on the Aggies' 2026 ledger, against the one team whose profile said they were overdue for a breakthrough.

They're 8-0 now. The best win on the season is no longer the 11-9 over Crush three weeks ago — it's this morning, against a team that was demonstrably the strongest they'd seen all spring. Eight games in, it's fair to stop calling the Aggies' start lucky. They've beaten good teams close, bad teams by run-rule, and now the best team in their division on its best day.

What it means for Alo
  • Aggies (8-0) clinch sole first with two games to play.
  • Comets (6-2) are done. Aggies own head-to-head after the 14-13 win — even if Aggies go 0-2 and Comets go 2-0, the division stays in Bunker Hill.
  • Bombers (5-1) are the only live mathematical threat. No Aggies-Bombers game on the regular-season schedule, so a Bombers title requires Aggies to slip AND non-H2H tiebreakers to fall their way.

Aggies close at Force Monday (6:00 PM, SWMS Field 2) and at home vs Storm next Saturday. Win both and it's 10-0 clean.

The easy version of this story says Bunker Hill kept doing what they've done all spring. The real version is that they didn't — they got pushed, they got outplayed for stretches, and they still won. 8-0 isn't a hot start anymore. It's an identity.

Division Watch Week of 4/18 · 6U Finch · Two Still Standing

Two Unbeaten. One Week to Meet.

Sliders 7-0 vs 5-0 Hurricanes Sat 4/25 · 3:30 PM · SWMS Field 3

With two-thirds of the Finch field now below .500, the top of the standings has thinned to exactly two teams — the Sliders and the Hurricanes — and they share a date on the calendar.

The Sliders are the bulldozer: +51 run differential, a league-best 96 runs scored in seven games, and wins over Venom and Magic that graded out as genuine tests. The Hurricanes have played it quieter but tighter — only 41 runs allowed in 5 games, which is the best rate in Finch by a full run per game.

Tonight at 5:00 PM on SWMS Field 3, the Hurricanes get Venom (4-2) — the strongest opponent they've faced in three weeks. It's a tune-up with stakes: stumble here, and the Sliders walk into the 4/25 showdown already looking like the team to beat.

What it means for Finch
  • Sliders (7-0) have three games left and the shorter path — Cowgirls tomorrow, Hurricanes on 4/25, Fury on 4/29.
  • Hurricanes (5-0) have five games left, the toughest stretch in the division: Venom tonight, Dragons Sunday, Aces Monday, then Sliders next Saturday.
  • Fury, Venom, Space Cowgirls (all 4-2) need chaos up top and clean sweeps of their own remaining slates to crash the party.

The Sliders have already survived their toughest stretch. The Hurricanes' starts tonight. The scoreboards between now and 4/25 will tell you which one of them is actually a #1 seed.

Division Watch Week of 4/18 · 6U Chamberlain · Three Undefeated

Three Unbeaten. 96 Hours to Sort It Out.

Diamonds 5-0 · Frogs 5-0 · Typhoons 4-0-1

Three teams, no losses, and the schedule is about to pick a winner for us.

The Diamonds run the defense — 20 runs allowed in 5 games, a number no team in any 6U division can match. The Frogs run the balance — +24 differential against the toughest slate any unbeaten has seen. The Typhoons sit in third only because of a tie, not a loss, and have the league's second-best run prevention (35 RA in 5).

The Diamonds draw both of the other two — @ Typhoons Sunday 2:30 PM (SWMS Field 3), then @ Frogs Tuesday 6:00 PM (SWMS Field 2). Two road games, 48 hours apart, against the only teams in the division that haven't lost. Sunday's matchup is the site's Game of the Week.

What it means for Chamberlain
  • Diamonds @ Typhoons (Sun 4/19) — best defense vs second-best defense. Lowest-scoring game on the board this weekend.
  • Diamonds @ Frogs (Tue 4/21) — whoever wins is the last unbeaten left and effectively takes the division.
  • Today's tune-ups: Diamonds host Chargers at 12:30, Frogs travel to Oilers at 2:00, Typhoons visit Firecrackers at 12:30. No bracket-busters expected — but injuries, blowouts, or rainouts reshape next week.

Five weeks of unbeaten baseball. One 96-hour window to crown it.

Playoff preview
What the rulebook says: The SBMSA 6U/8U rulebook (Rev. Spring 2026) references a "Gold Division" as the top playoff tier — implying multiple playoff brackets with the top teams re-seeded into the Gold group. What the rulebook doesn't say: the bracket format, the seeding criteria, or exactly which teams qualify for Gold Division. Those details aren't published in the official document. Treat the read below as inference, not league-official.

If Gold Division takes the top seeds out of each division (the most common format, and the only one consistent with a "Gold" label), the field starts to look like this:

  • Alo locks: Aggies. Second Alo seed comes down to Bombers (5-1) and Comets (6-2), who don't play again in the regular season.
  • Finch locks: Sliders. Second Finch seed is the Hurricanes' to lose — their 5-0 record is cleaner than any team behind them, though Venom (4-2), Space Cowgirls (4-2), and Fury (4-2) are all a couple of Hurricanes stumbles away from the conversation.
  • Chamberlain locks: none yet. Three unbeatens. The Diamonds' 96-hour road trip (@ Typhoons Sun, @ Frogs Tue) decides who goes in as #1 — and almost certainly clears up who the #2 is.
Final two weeks — what actually matters
  • Sun 4/19, 2:30 PM · Diamonds @ Typhoons (SWMS Field 3) — Game of the Week. Two unbeatens, the division's two best defenses.
  • Tue 4/21, 6:00 PM · Diamonds @ Frogs (SWMS Field 2) — Whoever wins this is the last Chamberlain unbeaten. Effectively the Chamberlain #1 decider.
  • Sat 4/25, 3:30 PM · Sliders @ Hurricanes (SWMS Field 3) — Finch #1 seed. Both teams bring unbeaten records in.
  • Sat 4/25, 9:30 AM · Storm @ Aggies (SWMS Field 4) — Aggies' last regular-season game. A win closes the book on Alo at 10-0.

Three divisions. Six unbeatens entering the weekend. By the end of next Saturday, we'll know how many are still standing — and whose bracket this actually is.
